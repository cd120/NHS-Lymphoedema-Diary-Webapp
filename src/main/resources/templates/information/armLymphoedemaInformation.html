Arm lymphoedema

Arm is one of the most usual parts which will have lymphoedema. It's not possible to completely prevent lymphoedema, but the following advice may help reduce your chances of developing it.

Guides

Taking Measurements

We ask that you measure at 7 different points on your hand and arm, please familiarise yourself with the pictorial guide as this will help determine your measuring points

  • measure A is the base of the fingers
  • measure B is at the base of the thumb web
  • measure C is the wrist
  • measure D is the mid forearm
  • measure E is the elbow slightly bent
  • measure F is the mid upper arm
  • measure G is at the top of the arm level with the armpit

Try and measure at the same time of the day on the same day once a week

Support your arm on a flat surface eg a table top with palm facing upwards (see picture)

Please record baseline measures prior to starting the compression wrap treatment -

ideally we would like you to measure in the same 7 places each week, this can be done using steps 1 and 2

Step 1 - determine your 7 measuring points using the marker pen (the marks can be washed off after youâ€™ve measured each time) these points are at the base of the fingers (measure A), base of the thumb web (measure B), wrist (measure C), mid forearm (measure D), elbow (slightly bent, measure E) mid upper arm (measure F) and top of arm (measure G)

Step 2- using a tape measure record the length from the wrist (measure C) to the mark you have chosen as measure D (mid forearm) and document that length at the top of the measurements and on the pictorial sheet, continue to record the lengths from the wrist to the elbow (measure E), then wrist to mid upper arm (measure F) and finally wrist to top of the arm (measure G) by doing this you will be able to ensure that you are measuring at a similar point each week

Step 3 - measure the circumference at each of the 7 points and record them on the '1st day' row in the appropriate column

Step 4 - apply compression hand and arm wraps as guided by the manufacturers booklet. Wraps are to be worn day and night, removing for showering, skin inspection, hand washing

Step 5 - measure the 7 points each week
