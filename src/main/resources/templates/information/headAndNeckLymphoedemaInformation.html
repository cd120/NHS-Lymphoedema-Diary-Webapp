Head and Neck Lymphoedema

What is Head and Neck Lymphoedema?

Head and neck lymphoedema is a swelling that occurs in the soft tissues of the head and neck due to an impaired lymphatic system. This may result from treatments such as surgery or radiation that affect the lymph nodes, leading to fluid retention and tissue swelling. Understanding and managing this condition is crucial, especially for those who have undergone cancer treatments.

More about Head and Neck Lymphoedema

Lymphoedema can lead to a variety of symptoms including a feeling of heaviness, tightness in the skin, and decreased flexibility. In severe cases, it may also cause discomfort or pain. This condition requires ongoing management to prevent worsening and complications such as infections.

Causes

Head and neck lymphoedema often occurs as a secondary condition following treatment for head and neck cancers. These treatments can damage the lymphatic system, impairing its ability to drain fluid effectively. Other causes may include infections, injuries, or congenital abnormalities that affect the lymphatic system.

Symptoms

  • Swelling in the face, neck, or around the eyes
  • A feeling of fullness or tightness in the affected areas
  • Changes in the texture of the skin
  • Reduced flexibility in the neck or jaw
  • Difficulty with daily activities due to discomfort or swelling

Treatment and Management

Skincare

Maintaining proper skincare is vital in managing lymphoedema. The affected area is more prone to infections, so keeping the skin clean and moisturized is essential. Regular checks for any signs of infection, such as redness or increased warmth, should be conducted.

Compression Garments

Compression garments help reduce swelling by applying consistent pressure to the affected areas, promoting fluid drainage. Proper fitting and regular use of these garments are key components of managing lymphoedema.

Exercise

Gentle exercises can help stimulate lymph flow, reduce swelling, and maintain flexibility. Your healthcare provider can recommend specific exercises tailored to your condition.

Manual Lymphatic Drainage (MLD)

MLD is a specialized massage technique used to stimulate lymph flow and reduce swelling. It is often performed by trained therapists and can be an important part of your treatment plan.
