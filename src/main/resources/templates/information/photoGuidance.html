Photo Guidance

Tips on taking photos with your smart phone

  • Have a solid background like a plain wall or plain sheet. Avoid distractions in the background like television, cushions etc.
  • Good lighting is essential. Use good light like sunlight or the flash on the camera with no shadows.
  • After taking the photo zoom in to see if the picture is in focus. If you cannot see it clearly, then neither can we. Please make sure that the image is not blurred.

For areas affected by oedema

  • Take a photo 2 feet away from the area so that we can see its location on your body.
  • Take a second close up photo of the same area about one foot away, so we can see details of the area.
  • Please do not send images of genital or breast area; if this is the location of your skin problem then there is no need to send a photo, we will be in touch in due course with a face to face appointment.

For lesions/skin changes

  • Take a photo 2 feet away from the lesion so that we can see its location on your body.
  • Take a second close up photo of the same lesion about one foot away, so we can see details of the lesion
  • Use a ruler or a coin next to the lesion (but not obscuring it) to give us a sense of the size of the lesion.
  • For subtle lesions please use a pen to draw an arrow pointing to the lesion
  • Please do not send images of genital or breast area; if this is the location of your skin problem then there is no need to send a photo and we will be in touch in due course with a face to face appointment.

For rashes

  • Take a picture of the entire rash
  • Take a close up of the worst areas
  • Not more than 5 photos need to be sent. Usually 2 or 3 will be enough.
  • Try to imagine the body divided into segments and take photos of the segments if the rash is widespread.
  • Please do not send images of genital or breast area; if this is the location of your skin problem then there is no need to send a photo, we will be in touch in due course with a face to face appointment.

Information sources: NHS Health Guide, University Hospital of Derby and Burton - Lymphoedema Service Patient Information
