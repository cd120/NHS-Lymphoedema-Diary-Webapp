Healthy Eating and Lymphoedema

Lymphoedema is a condition which is made worse by being overweight. Carrying excess weight can also make treatment for lymphoedema less effective. Lymph nodes and a complex network of lymphatic vessels are found directly under the skin’s surface. The presence of excess fat impairs the normal functioning of the lymphatic vessels, probably by reducing the effect of contracting muscles which help to push fluid through them. Losing weight may not only have a beneficial effect on response to treatment but also reduce your risk of other conditions related to being overweight, such as heart disease and stroke. Weight loss may also reduce the volume of your limb, by reducing the fat in it, and increase your energy levels. If you have any queries regarding whether you should follow healthy eating and/or if you should lose weight, then please talk to your lymphoedema nurse/therapist.

What is healthy eating?

Healthy eating should be seen as a long term change. It is not a ‘diet’ and there are no quick fixes. Healthy eating can be followed by anyone regardless of age. Try to be realistic about the changes that you make and alter your eating habits gradually. This ultimately makes it easier for you to keep to the new pattern and improves results.

The Eatwell Guide

There are 5 food groups and choosing a variety of foods from each group will provide the body with the nutrients it requires. Foods from the group containing fat and containing sugar are not essential to a healthy diet and should form the smallest part of the diet.

The 5 main food groups

Fruit and Vegetables

Aim to have at least 5 portions of fruit and vegetables a day. This can include fresh, tinned, frozen, fresh juice or dried products.

Foods rich in carbohydrates and fibre

These include bread, potatoes, cereals, pasta and rice. Try to choose wholegrain products where possible.

Foods containing protein

These include lean meat (red and white), fish e.g. cod, haddock (including oily fish e.g. mackerel, salmon, sardines), eggs, beans and pulses.

Milk and dairy foods

Try to choose low fat options such as low fat cheese, semi-skimmed or skimmed milk, diet yogurts.

Foods containing fat and foods containing sugar

Fat is a source of calories, so it is important to reduce saturated fats such as those found in cheese, butter, crisps as well as unsaturated fats found in vegetable oils, salad dressings. Try to choose reduced fat spread, reduced fat salad dressings. Take care not to add additional fat to foods during cooking e.g. frying in oil, adding butter/margarine, having rich sauces.

Sugary foods also tend to be high in calories. Try to minimise your intake of sugary food such as cakes, biscuits, chocolate, fizzy pop. Sweeteners can be used instead of sugar and diet fizzy pop or reduced sugar varieties can be chosen instead.
