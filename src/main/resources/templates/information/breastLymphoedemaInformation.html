Breast lymphoedema

Breast lymphoedema is a common condition that can develop after treatment for breast cancer. It is important to recognize the symptoms and seek early intervention to manage the condition effectively.

Symptoms

Symptoms of breast lymphoedema include swelling, a feeling of heaviness or tightness in the breast, changes in the texture of the skin, and redness. These symptoms can develop gradually and may worsen over time if not managed properly.

Diagnosis

Breast lymphoedema is typically diagnosed through physical examination, patient history, and imaging tests such as lymphoscintigraphy. Healthcare providers may also use measurements of the affected area to assess the severity of the condition.

Risk Factors

Risk factors for developing breast lymphoedema include:

  • Extensive surgery or radiation therapy to the breast and lymph nodes
  • Infection or injury to the lymphatic system
  • Being overweight or obese
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Pre-existing venous conditions

Management and Treatment

Management of breast lymphoedema includes manual lymphatic drainage (MLD), the use of compression garments, and regular exercise. In some cases, surgical options may be considered. Early intervention and consistent management are crucial in reducing the impact of the condition.

Self-Care and Exercises

Self-Care and Exercises

Engaging in regular self-care practices is essential in managing breast lymphoedema. This includes:

  • Performing daily exercises to encourage lymphatic flow
  • Maintaining good skin care to prevent infections
  • Wearing compression garments as prescribed by your healthcare provider
  • Regular monitoring of the affected area for any changes
